About Daniel Shim

My Design History

Hello everyone! My name is Daniel Shim. I am living in South Korea. I am capable of speaking english and korean. I have made this website to briefly describe and share about my design history.

Since I was about 3-4 years old I started to play with toys called Playmobil. It was a toy that taught me the things in our world. Like for example, cars, houses, buses, trains, planes, and buildings were the things I learned through playmobil.

But since playmobil was just for very young kids I was bored with playmobil when I was at an age of 5. So my parents introduced me to a new world of Lego. Lego helped me learn the reality, increased my creativity, and taught how the world works. In addition, I have developed my thoughts and designs. But it also made memories with my brother. He and I made lego together. We had good memories together.

After a while, my parents introduced me to a craft studio. I was able to design and build anything I wanted. I was able to build house models, dolls, miniatures, and so many other craft designs. The studio recycled materials for the kids to make projects. I used a lot of cardboard boxes, needles, fabric, and etc. I was able to make my own creative world where my own thoughts and ideas were represented.

When I entered 5th grade I was starting to develop a new sense of design ideas. I learned about computer coding/programming. I was able to develop web designs where I can also show my thoughts and ideas just like the craft studio. I really enjoyed learning about these new fascinating worlds. I was also introduced to Minecraft which is a designing game for developing your own world. This helped me get interested in housing, interior design, and architect too.

Now I am still enjoying lego, Minecraft, coding, and crafting. Through designing I learned and developed my own creativity and my own sense of looking at the world. Next time I would like to find out new design ideas where I can share my thoughts and ideas creatively in my own way. Thank you! Bye!
